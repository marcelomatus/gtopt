# Convenio del Maule: Research Summary

## Irrigation and Hydroelectric Water Rights on the Maule River System

**Research Date:** April 2026

---

## 1. Historical Context

### 1.1 The 1947 Convention (Convenio de 1947)

The foundational agreement governing water use in the Laguna del Maule was signed
in 1947 between the Director of the Departamento de Riego (Irrigation Department,
predecessor of the DOH) and the General Manager of Endesa (then a state-owned
company). The purpose was to regulate the joint use of the Laguna del Maule
reservoir for both irrigation and hydroelectric generation.

Key background: Endesa negotiated participation in the expansion of the dam that
the Direccion de Riego was undertaking with financing from farmers. The resulting
arrangement transformed the Laguna del Maule into a **multipurpose reservoir**
serving both irrigation and power generation.

Under the 1947 Convention, approximately **80% of the waters belong to the State**,
administered by the Ministry of Public Works (MOP) through the Direccion de Obras
Hidraulicas (DOH), with irrigation as the priority use. The Convention explicitly
stated that Endesa could use the waters of the Laguna del Maule **"without altering
the development proposed for irrigation"**.

The total capacity of the Laguna del Maule was defined as approximately **1,570
million cubic meters (Hm3)**.

### 1.2 The Three Portions of Laguna del Maule

The 1947 Convention divides the Laguna del Maule into three portions, each with
different water use regulations:

1. **Upper Portion (Porcion Superior):** Approximately 170 Hm3. Subject to
   specific rules for extraordinary use.

2. **Intermediate/Ordinary Portion (Porcion Intermedia):** Approximately 500 Hm3.
   This is the most contested portion. According to the Convention, water in this
   portion is allocated with a proportion of **80% for irrigation and 20% for
   electricity generation** by Endesa (now Enel).

3. **Lower Portion (Porcion Inferior):** The base volume. Rules differ regarding
   what "economies" (water savings) can be extracted from this level.

A central legal dispute has revolved around whether Endesa/Enel can extract
"economies" (excess water) from the intermediate portion, or only from the upper
portion when the lagoon is full enough to reach it.

### 1.3 The 1983 Convention (Colbun-Machicura)

With the commissioning of the Colbun-Machicura hydroelectric complex, a new
convention was signed in 1983 between Endesa and the DGA (Direccion General de
Aguas). This convention established **non-consumptive water rights** on the Maule
River in favor of Endesa, allowing a mean annual flow of **190 m3/s** with a
maximum of **280 m3/s**.

The Colbun reservoir (capacity ~1,500 Hm3) and the downstream Machicura reservoir
became critical infrastructure in the system, operating both for hydroelectric
generation and as potential water storage for irrigation purposes.

## 2. Key Provisions and Institutional Framework

### 2.1 Parties Involved

- **Endesa / Enel Chile:** Successor company holding the original water rights
  from the 1947 Convention. Operates the Pehuenche hydroelectric complex
  (Pehuenche and Loma Alta plants) downstream of Laguna del Maule. Also
  developing the Los Condores hydroelectric project (150 MW).

- **Colbun S.A.:** Operator of the Colbun-Machicura hydroelectric complex on the
  Maule River. Owns the Colbun and Machicura reservoirs.

- **Direccion de Obras Hidraulicas (DOH):** Government agency within the MOP
  responsible for administering the State's water stored in Laguna del Maule for
  irrigation purposes (successor of the Departamento de Riego).

- **Junta de Vigilancia del Rio Maule (JVRM):** The river oversight board
  representing irrigators and water rights holders in the Maule basin. Key
  negotiator on behalf of agricultural users.

- **Asociacion Canal Maule (ACM):** Canal operator serving irrigators in the
  region.

### 2.2 Priority of Irrigation Over Generation

The Convention of 1947 and subsequent court rulings establish a clear hierarchy:
**irrigation takes priority over electricity generation** in the use of water from
the Laguna del Maule. Only surplus water above irrigation needs can be used for
hydroelectric purposes.

### 2.3 The "Convenio de Ahorro Hidrico" (Water Savings Agreement)

Starting in 2008, a separate operational agreement was developed between the JVRM,
Colbun S.A., and Pehuenche S.A. (Enel) to manage seasonal water use:

- **Mechanism:** During spring (September-December), when rains and snowmelt
  provide abundant water, irrigators voluntarily restrict their usage. The "saved"
  water is stored in the **Colbun and Machicura reservoirs** (owned by the
  hydroelectric companies).

- **Purpose:** This saved water is then returned to irrigators during the summer
  months (January-April), when river flows diminish and irrigation demand peaks.

- **Duration evolution:** Initially negotiated annually (with significant tension
  each year), the agreement was later extended to **five-year terms**, providing
  greater planning stability for both irrigators and generators.

- **Water toll (peaje):** The savings "toll" (payment in water for using
  hydroelectric reservoirs as storage) was originally 50% and has been improved to
  **70% return to irrigators**, meaning farmers pay only 30% for using the
  Pehuenche reservoir (Enel) as storage.

- **Return period:** The period for returning saved water was expanded from
  January-February to **January through April**, reflecting the reality that
  climate change is pushing irrigation needs later in the season.

- **Impact:** The agreement has provided approximately **400 million cubic meters
  of extra water** for irrigation over its lifetime.

- **Ten-year renewal:** The Assembly of the JVRM approved a **10-year renewal** of
  the savings agreement with Colbun and Pehuenche.

## 3. Role of the Colbun Reservoir

The Colbun reservoir plays a central role in the water management system of the
Maule basin:

1. **Seasonal storage for irrigation:** Under the Convenio de Ahorro, Colbun
   serves as a "savings bank" for irrigation water during spring, to be withdrawn
   during summer. This is operationally critical because Laguna del Maule is at
   high altitude and subject to different release constraints.

2. **Buffer capacity:** With a capacity of approximately 1,500 Hm3, Colbun
   provides substantial storage that complements the Laguna del Maule (1,570 Hm3).

3. **Dual-use infrastructure:** The reservoir serves both Colbun S.A.'s
   hydroelectric generation needs and the irrigation community's seasonal storage
   requirements, making the Convenio de Ahorro possible.

4. **Downstream position:** Located downstream of the Laguna del Maule and the
   Pehuenche complex, Colbun captures flows that transit through the system,
   making it a strategic point for water management.

## 4. Legal Disputes and Key Court Rulings

### 4.1 The 2012-2021 Legal Battle

In 2012, the Consejo de Defensa del Estado (CDE, State Defense Council) initiated
a "judgment action for certainty" (juicio de certeza) to clarify the
interpretation of the 1947 Convention regarding how "economies" (water savings)
could be extracted:

- **State/CDE position:** Economies could only be made effective when the Laguna
  del Maule was in its **upper portion** (i.e., near full capacity).

- **Enel position:** Economies could also be extracted from the **intermediate
  (ordinary) portion**.

### 4.2 Supreme Court Ruling (March 17, 2021)

The Corte Suprema (Third Chamber) issued a landmark ruling:

- Dismissed Enel's cassation appeals against the lower court decision.
- **Confirmed that irrigation has preeminence** over electricity generation under
  the 1947 Convention.
- Ruled that the use of water from Laguna del Maule is **primarily for
  irrigation**, and only surpluses can be used for electricity generation.
- After **eight years of litigation**, this ruling reinforced the priority of
  irrigators.

Senator Ximena Rincon described the decision as "a resounding triumph" for the
agricultural community.

### 4.3 Congressional Investigation (2023)

The Chilean Chamber of Deputies (Camara de Diputados) formed a **Special
Investigative Commission** to examine possible violations of the 1947 Convention
regarding Enel's use of water from the Laguna del Maule.

In July 2023, the Commission approved its conclusions:

- The report examined evidence about who authorized Enel to use water from the
  Laguna del Maule for purposes other than irrigation.
- The approved report (Deputy Donoso's proposal, 4-1-1 vote) concluded that, given
  the lagoon's water levels had not reached the upper portion, there would be no
  obligation for the DOH to return water, and therefore **no formal violation** of
  the 1947 Convention had occurred under the strict interpretation.

### 4.4 Los Condores Hydroelectric Project

In 2014, Endesa Chile and the JVRM signed an agreement to enable the construction
and operation of the **Los Condores hydroelectric plant (150 MW)**, involving an
investment of approximately **US$660 million**:

- The agreement was signed on February 28, 2014.
- It established mechanisms for water operation that would not modify the general
  regulation regime of the river and the Laguna del Maule.
- However, controversy arose when farmers learned that Endesa had separately signed
  an agreement with the DOH to gain greater control over the **gates (compuertas)
  of the Laguna del Maule**, leading to significant farmer opposition.
- The MOP issued a resolution that was later characterized as having "confusingly
  returned waters to Endesa."

## 5. Recent Developments (2024-2026)

### 5.1 Laguna del Maule Recovery

During 2024, the Laguna del Maule exceeded the **670 Hm3 accumulated limit** for
the first time in 14 years, moving out of the intermediate portion where it had
been stuck. This was seen as a significant milestone in lagoon recovery efforts.

### 5.2 2026 New Operational Proposal

In January 2026, the JVRM presented a new operational proposal for the Laguna del
Maule to the DOH and Enel, structured around **five fundamental pillars**:

- The proposal considers **substantial changes** to how the lagoon has been
  historically operated, incorporating new measures.
- It aims to complement irrigation and optimize water use for all basin users.
- A **Technical Laguna del Maule Working Table (Mesa Tecnica)** was established as
  a joint work instance between the JVRM, DOH, and Enel.
- The third session of this Technical Table focused on agreeing an **Operating
  Model** for the reservoir based on "clear, verifiable and sustainable rules that
  contribute to greater water security."
- The changes must be evaluated, adjusted, and agreed upon between the three
  parties before implementation.

### 5.3 Climate Change and Water Security

The broader context for recent negotiations is the impact of climate change on the
Maule basin:

- Extended drought has reduced water availability.
- The Coordinador Electrico Nacional (National Electricity Coordinator) has warned
  about potential energy rationing in parts of the Maule region due to
  transmission limitations.
- There are calls for urgent agreements to safeguard the hydric future of the
  region.
- The JVRM is leading technical coordination to adapt reservoir operations to
  climate variability scenarios.

### 5.4 Infrastructure Investments

The Comision Nacional de Riego (CNR) and the Regional Government of Maule have
continued investing in irrigation infrastructure through Law No. 18,450
(Irrigation Promotion Law), with projects in communes including Colbun and
Longavi benefiting over 145 farmers.

## 6. Key Takeaways for Power System Modeling

For GTEP (Generation and Transmission Expansion Planning) modeling:

1. **Operational constraints:** Hydroelectric plants in the Maule system
   (Pehuenche, Colbun-Machicura, Los Condores) face **binding irrigation
   constraints** that limit water available for generation, especially during
   January-April.

2. **Seasonal water transfers:** The Convenio de Ahorro creates a seasonal water
   transfer mechanism where water is stored in hydroelectric reservoirs during
   spring and released for irrigation in summer -- effectively reducing generation
   capacity during peak irrigation demand.

3. **Reservoir operation rules:** The three-portion system of the Laguna del Maule
   creates non-trivial operating rules that affect how much water Enel can use for
   generation at different reservoir levels.

4. **Legal risk:** Ongoing disputes and evolving agreements mean that the
   operational parameters for these plants may change, creating uncertainty for
   long-term planning.

5. **Climate sensitivity:** The system is highly sensitive to drought conditions,
   which affect both irrigation demand and generation capacity simultaneously.
