\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:conclusions}

A reconstruction methodology for per-bus, per-quarter marginal
CO$_2$ emission factors in the Chilean power system has been
presented.  The pipeline, distributed as the \texttt{cen2gtopt}
subpackage of \texttt{gtopt}, combines the SCVIC merit-ladder, the
published \texttt{generaci\'on-real} dispatch, the
\texttt{instrucciones-operacionales-cmg} price-taker classification,
the PLEXOS PID overlay for binding technical-minimum data, and the
PCP/PID solution \texttt{.accdb} oracle, into a pipeline that runs
on a warm cache in approximately \SI{6}{\second}.  Across the
\num{2494} (bus, quarter) cells of 2026-04-22 the reconstructed
marginal cost agrees with $\lambda^{\mathrm{CEN}}$ to a mean
absolute error of \SI{0.152}{\USD\per\MWh}, a P90 of
\SI{0.471}{\USD\per\MWh}, and a maximum of
\SI{1.338}{\USD\per\MWh}; per-island marginal-unit coherence is
exact (\num{0}/\num{474} disagreements).

The pipeline closes a documented gap: CEN does not publish a per-bus
per-quarter marginal CO$_2$ factor, and prior academic work has
focused on system-level or zonal aggregates~\cite{Bonvallet2016,
Moreno2014}.  This is, to our knowledge, the first public-data-only
reconstruction at \SI{15}{\minute} resolution validated against the
LP-solver-output values extracted from the PLEXOS \texttt{.accdb},
and the first openly distributed pipeline for the SEN with a
hash-keyed cache and multi-day Hive-partitioned orchestration.

Three forward integrations are envisaged.  First, a multi-day rollout
of the \texttt{cen2gtopt-marginal-period} orchestrator on a daily
cron with public artefact publishing — closing the operator-facing
gap relative to CAISO's \textit{Today's
Outlook}~\cite{CAISOOutlook}.  Second, a CEN public dashboard
(Streamlit/Flask over the parquet schema) that exposes today's
$\lambda$, $\varepsilon$, and island map to non-Python consumers;
the schema is already DuckDB-friendly.  Third, a counterfactual
companion via \texttt{gtopt} stochastic dual dynamic
programming~\cite{gtopt2026}: feeding the same SCVIC ladder, the
same dispatch, and the same MLFs into the SDDP solver would produce
a per-bus marginal loss + congestion decomposition at the cost of
one auxiliary LP per perturbation, calibrating the empirical
reverse-engineering's ground truth in the spirit of
\cite{ValenzuelaASL2022}.  This third integration also opens a
back-test window for the SDDP solver itself: any divergence between
the gtopt-computed $\hat\lambda^{\mathrm{SDDP}}$ and the empirical
$\hat\lambda$ on a closed historical day flags a solver/data
discrepancy worth investigating.

The pipeline source, the parquet outputs for the headline day, and
the figures of this paper are available at
\url{https://github.com/marcelomatus/gtopt}; \texttt{cen2gtopt}
installs via \texttt{pip} from the \texttt{scripts/} directory.
