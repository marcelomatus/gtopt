\section{Methodology}
\label{sec:methodology}

The pipeline is structured as a directed acyclic graph of nine
stages (Fig.~\ref{fig:pipeline}); each stage is a pure function of
its predecessors, allowing the parquet cache to short-circuit any
upstream change.

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figures/fig_pipeline_flow.pdf}
  \caption{The nine-step \texttt{cen2gtopt.marginal\_units}
  pipeline.  Hash-keyed parquet caching wraps every CEN/SCVIC/
  PLEXOS extractor; each downstream stage is a pure function of its
  inputs.}
  \label{fig:pipeline}
\end{figure}

\subsection{System Model and Notation}
\label{sec:notation}

Time is partitioned into a hierarchy of \emph{operating days},
\emph{hours}, and \emph{quarters} (\SI{15}{\minute} blocks).  Within
one operating day let $\setT=\{0,\ldots,95\}$ index quarters and
$\setH=\{0,\ldots,23\}$ index hours so that each $h\in\setH$
contains the four quarters $\{4h,4h{+}1,4h{+}2,4h{+}3\}$.  The
network exposes a set of buses $\setN$ at which CEN publishes a
nodal marginal cost $\lambda^{\mathrm{CEN}}_{b,t}$ for $b\in\setN,
t\in\setT$.  The dispatchable generator set $\setG$ is indexed by
the canonical CEN identifier $g\equiv\texttt{id\_central}$; each
generator has a fuel label $f(g)$, a technical minimum
$\underline{p}_g$, an effective maximum $\overline{p}_g$, and a
piecewise SCVIC ladder $\Phi_g=\{\phi\}$ of declared variable
costs $c_{g,\phi}$.  Per-hour realised dispatch is denoted
$p_{g,h}\in\R_+$.

The pipeline produces, per (bus, quarter), the seven primary
quantities
\[
\lambda^{\mathrm{CEN}}_{b,t},\quad
\hat\lambda_{b,t},\quad
m_{b,t}\in\setG,\quad
c_{m,\phi^\star},\quad
\varepsilon_{m},\quad
\mathrm{LF}_{b,t},\quad
\varepsilon^{\mathrm{bus}}_{b,t},
\]
where $\hat\lambda_{b,t}$ is the reconstructed marginal price,
$m_{b,t}$ is the marginal unit, $c_{m,\phi^\star}$ its picked CV,
$\varepsilon_m$ its fuel-default emission factor (in
$\si{\tonne\mathrm{CO}_2\per\MWh}$), $\mathrm{LF}_{b,t}$ the bus
loss factor, and $\varepsilon^{\mathrm{bus}}_{b,t} =
\varepsilon_m \cdot \mathrm{LF}_{b,t}$ the bus-level marginal
emission factor at the quarter.

\subsection{Bus Catalogue and UID Bridging}
\label{sec:bridge}

The CEN data triplet uses three different bus identifiers.  The SIP
APIs publish a stable integer \texttt{id\_info} together with a
17-character canonical key (\texttt{bar\_transf}, e.g.\
\texttt{CRUCERO\_\_\_\_\_\_\_220}) and a free-text human label
(\texttt{barra\_info}, e.g.\ \texttt{BA S/E CRUCERO 220KV BP1}).
The SCVIC parquet exposes a numeric power-station ID and free-text
plant/configuration names; the PLEXOS XML/\texttt{.accdb} bundle
uses CamelCase node names like \texttt{Crucero220}.

The \texttt{\_id\_bridge.py} module reconciles these via a
two-stage process.  A normalisation step folds Unicode
diacritics, strips punctuation, and lower-cases each name.  A
generator function then produces up to six aliases per name by
stripping common Chilean prefixes (\texttt{TER},
\texttt{TERMOEL\'ECTRICA}, \texttt{CENTRAL}) and unit suffixes
(\texttt{BL1}-\texttt{BL9}, \texttt{TG}, \texttt{TV},
\texttt{CCGT}); two passes converge on multi-suffix names.  A
hand-curated overrides table (\texttt{PLEXOS\_TO\_BAR\_TRANSF} in
\texttt{prepopulate\_cache.py}) reconciles the residual cases that
the heuristic cannot resolve, predominantly buses where the
PLEXOS topology aggregates two physical substations into one
node.  The cache contains \num{29} reconciled production buses
out of the \num{1300}+ CEN-published buses; only the
\SI{220}{\kV} and \SI{500}{\kV} backbone is included by default
because the lower-voltage buses share the same nodal price as their
backbone parent in CEN's PLEXOS topology.

\subsection{Operational-State Classification}
\label{sec:classifier}

Each generator at each hour falls into one of eight mutually
exclusive operational states; the priority-ordered rule table is
documented in Table~\ref{tab:classifier}.  The classification
matters because price-taker units (those held at a
technical-minimum or operator-imposed setpoint) cannot supply the
\textit{next} \SI{1}{\MWh} of energy and therefore cannot set the
marginal price even when their declared CV is closest to
$\lambda$.  The classifier reads the
\texttt{instrucciones-operacionales-cmg} feed and flags as forced
any unit with \texttt{estado}$\ne$\texttt{N} or
\texttt{consigna}$\in$\{\texttt{CI}, \texttt{MT}, \texttt{PMT},
\texttt{PP}, \texttt{PS}, \texttt{PC}\}.

  \caption{Operational-state classification.  Priority is evaluated
  top-down: the first matching row determines the unit's status at
  the hour.}
  \label{tab:classifier}
  \input{tables/tab_classifier.tex}

A subtle rule extends the classical PJM treatment: forced units
\emph{at interior dispatch} ($\underline{p}_g + \delta < p_g <
\overline{p}_g - \delta$ with $\delta=0.05(\overline{p}_g -
\underline{p}_g)$) remain valid marginal-unit candidates because
the next \SI{1}{\MWh} can physically come from them; only forced
units \emph{at} or \emph{near} pmin are excluded.  This rule
is implemented vectorially in \texttt{identify\_marginal} and
typically retains \SIrange{20}{50}{\percent} of the forced units in
the candidate pool at gas-marginal hours.

\subsection{Island Clustering}
\label{sec:island}

Buses that share the same nodal marginal price in a given quarter
are electrically coherent in the merit-clearing sense.  The naive
approach of grouping buses with $|\lambda_b - \lambda_{b'}| \le
\Delta_{\mathrm{tol}}$ exhibits a chained-clustering pathology: a
sequence such as $(\$41.5, \$41.7, \$41.9, \$42.1, \$42.3, \$42.7)$
with $\Delta_{\mathrm{tol}}=\$0.5$ collapses to a single island
despite the endpoints differing by \$1.20.  Without correction,
this lumps congested zones together with the system price and
defeats the marginal-unit picker.

The pipeline uses a chained-tolerance walk with a \emph{cumulative
spread cap}.  After sorting the buses in a quarter by ascending
$\lambda$, a new island is started whenever
\begin{equation}
\lambda_b - \lambda_{b-1} > \Delta_{\mathrm{tol}}
\quad\lor\quad
\lambda_b - \lambda^{\mathrm{isl,min}} > S_{\max},
\label{eq:island}
\end{equation}
with $\Delta_{\mathrm{tol}} = \$0.5$/MWh and $S_{\max} = \$0.8$/MWh
in production.  Fig.~\ref{fig:island} contrasts the pure pairwise
walk (left, one island) with the cumulative-spread walk (right,
two islands).

\subsection{Per-bus Marginal Loss Factor Correction}
\label{sec:mlf}

The reconstructed price $\hat\lambda_{b,t}$ at the bus is the
product of the marginal-unit CV at the system reference price and
the bus loss factor:
\begin{equation}
\hat\lambda_{b,t} = c_{m_{b,t}, \phi^\star} \cdot \mathrm{LF}_{b,t}.
\label{eq:lambda-bus}
\end{equation}
The pipeline supports two estimators of $\mathrm{LF}_{b,t}$ in
priority order.  The \emph{primary} estimator reads the
PLEXOS-solver-output Marginal Loss Factor from the PID solution
\texttt{.accdb} for the day, attached to the
\texttt{System.Nodes} collection at \texttt{collection\_id=281}.
This is the exact value used at LP clearing; the
\texttt{pcp\_solution.py} module extracts it via
\texttt{mdbtools} and the bus-name bridge of
Section~\ref{sec:bridge} translates the PLEXOS node names to
\texttt{id\_info}.  The \emph{fallback} estimator is a per-quarter
empirical ratio
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{LF}^{\mathrm{emp}}_{b,t} = \frac{\lambda^{\mathrm{CEN}}_{b,t}}{\lambda^{\mathrm{CEN}}_{r,t}},
\label{eq:lf-emp}
\end{equation}
where the reference bus $r$ is the lowest-\texttt{id\_info} bus
present in the quarter (typically a centre-Santiago backbone bus
with the lowest losses).  The empirical estimator is
self-consistent at $\lambda$ but does not generalise to a
counterfactual; it is used only when the PID accdb is missing for
the day.  When both estimators are available, the ratio is
constructed as $\mathrm{LF}^{\mathrm{PID}}_{b,t} =
\mathrm{LF}^{\mathrm{bus}}/\mathrm{LF}^{\mathrm{unit}}$ so that
generator-side losses cancel and only the bus-relative loss
penalty remains.  The choice of median bus $\lambda$ (rather than
any single bus) as the picker target ensures that
loss factors of the islanded buses average to approximately unity,
addressing the inverse-MLF concern raised in
\cite{ValenzuelaASL2022}.

\subsection{SCVIC Merit-Ladder Construction}
\label{sec:scvic}

Each generator declares to the Coordinador a piecewise variable
cost $c_{g,\phi}$ as a function of configuration $\phi$ (fuel,
load tier, ambient).  Fig.~\ref{fig:ladder} illustrates two
representative ladders: a multi-tier GNL CCGT and a coal-fired
unit.  The pipeline filters tiers below a per-fuel CV floor to
exclude take-or-pay ``startup'' tiers
($c_{g,\phi}^{\min}=\SI{30}{\USD\per\MWh}$ for GNL, GLP, gas
propano; $\SI{15}{\USD\per\MWh}$ for all others) and tiers above
\SI{1000}{\USD\per\MWh} (PLEXOS sentinels for ``out of economic
order'').  All retained tiers per generator are stored as a sorted
list $\{c_{g,\phi}\}_\phi$ and used at picker time.

\subsection{Marginal-Unit Identification}
\label{sec:identify}

The picker is summarised in Algorithm~\ref{alg:identify}.  It
treats the candidate pool as the dispatched non-forced (or
forced-but-interior) units with at least one SCVIC tier, and picks
the unit whose nearest tier minimises $|c_{g,\phi}-\lambda|$.  The
returned record carries the regime label, which classifies the
output into one of seven values: \texttt{matched\_interior},
\texttt{matched\_capped\_pmax}, \texttt{matched\_near\_pmin},
\texttt{matched\_above}, \texttt{no\_candidates},
\texttt{no\_cv\_match}, \texttt{renewable\_curtailment}, or
\texttt{demand\_fail}.  These labels propagate to the output
parquet and are aggregated in the per-day diagnostic report.

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{Marginal-unit identification per island}
\label{alg:identify}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
  \REQUIRE $\lambda_{\mathrm{tgt}}$, candidate set $\setC$, ladders
           $\{\Phi_g\}$, fuel labels $\{f(g)\}$, forced flag
           $F_g\in\{0,1\}$
  \IF{$\lambda_{\mathrm{tgt}}\le\$0.5$}
    \STATE \RETURN regime $=$ \texttt{renewable\_curtailment},
           $\varepsilon=0$
  \ELSIF{$\lambda_{\mathrm{tgt}}>\$800$}
    \STATE \RETURN regime $=$ \texttt{demand\_fail},
           $\varepsilon=0$
  \ENDIF
  \STATE pool $\leftarrow \{g\in\setC : \Phi_g \ne \emptyset\}$
  \STATE annotate each $g$ with status\,$\in$\,
         \{\texttt{interior}, \texttt{capped\_pmax},
         \texttt{near\_pmin}\} via $\delta=0.05(\overline{p}_g-\underline{p}_g)$
  \STATE drop $\{g : F_g=1 \land \mathrm{status}=\texttt{near\_pmin}\}$
  \IF{pool empty}
    \STATE \RETURN regime $=$ \texttt{no\_cv\_match}
  \ENDIF
  \FOR{$g\in\mathrm{pool}$}
    \STATE $c_g \leftarrow$ tier in $\Phi_g$ closest to
           $\lambda_{\mathrm{tgt}}$
    \STATE prefer the largest $c\le\lambda_{\mathrm{tgt}}$ when one exists
  \ENDFOR
  \STATE $m \leftarrow \arg\min_{g\in\mathrm{pool}}
           |c_g - \lambda_{\mathrm{tgt}}|$
  \STATE \RETURN $m$, $c_m$, $f(m)$, regime $\in
           \{\texttt{matched\_interior},$ \texttt{matched\_capped\_pmax},
           \texttt{matched\_above},\ldots$\}$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\subsection{Marginal Emission Factor}
\label{sec:eps}

The fuel-default emission factor table $\varepsilon_f$
(Table~\ref{tab:fuelef}) is derived from
IPCC~2006~GL Vol.~2 Tab.~1.4 multiplied by typical SEN heat rates.
The bus-level marginal emission factor is
\begin{equation}
\varepsilon^{\mathrm{bus}}_{b,t} = \varepsilon_{f(m_{b,t})}
                                   \cdot \mathrm{LF}_{b,t},
\label{eq:eps-bus}
\end{equation}
and the embedded carbon-tax cost is
$\tau_{\mathrm{CO}_2}\varepsilon^{\mathrm{bus}}_{b,t}/1000$ in
$\si{\USD\per\MWh}$ with
$\tau_{\mathrm{CO}_2}=\SI{5}{\USD\per\tonne\mathrm{CO}_2}$ under
Law~20.780.

\begin{table}[t]
  \centering
  \caption{Fuel-default emission factor table $\varepsilon_f$
  (\si{\kg\mathrm{CO}_2\per\MWh}).  IPCC 2006 GL Vol.~2 Tab.~1.4
  multiplied by typical SEN heat rates.  Biomass and biogas are
  classified as IPCC biogenic-zero; cogeneration is a mixed
  fossil/biogenic estimate.}
  \label{tab:fuelef}
  \input{tables/tab_fuel_ef.tex}
\end{table}

A per-cell \texttt{tier\_uncertainty} flag is set to true whenever
the picked tier is more than \$2/MWh below the next-up CV in the
same unit's ladder (the $\$2$ threshold is consistent with typical
SCVIC discrete-tier gaps such as biomass$\to$gas).  The flag
identifies cells where the structural reconstruction error is
bounded by the wedge to the next tier; downstream consumers can
treat $\hat\lambda$ as a lower bound for the wedge-flagged cells.
On 2026-04-22 \SI{13.7}{\percent} of cells are wedge-flagged.

\subsection{Cross-Validation Against the PLEXOS Solution Oracle}
\label{sec:oracle}

The CEN PCP/PID solution \texttt{.accdb} is parsed via
\texttt{pcp\_solution.py}, exposing per-period system shadow
prices, per-unit dispatch, and per-bus MLFs as Pandas DataFrames.
Three quantities are read for cross-validation: the per-bus
$\lambda$ from the PLEXOS solver (compared against
$\lambda^{\mathrm{CEN}}$ to validate the publication chain), the
per-unit dispatch $p_{g,h}^{\mathrm{PLEXOS}}$ (compared against
the SIP \texttt{generaci\'on-real} feed), and the per-bus
\texttt{Marginal Loss Factor} read from
\texttt{collection\_id=281} on the \texttt{System.Nodes}
collection.  When the same day has both a PCP and a PID bundle
on disk, both are loaded and the PID values take precedence —
the intra-day re-clear is the binding settlement reference.  The
oracle also yields the post-hoc $\hat\lambda^{\mathrm{PID}}_{b,t}$
column in the output parquet, computed using
$\mathrm{LF}^{\mathrm{PID}}_{b,t}$ in
\eqref{eq:lambda-bus} instead of the empirical ratio of
\eqref{eq:lf-emp}.

\subsection{Standards-Alignment Audit}
\label{sec:best-practices}

To clarify how the reconstruction methodology positions itself with
respect to the standards landscape reviewed in
Section~\ref{sec:literature-extended}, an explicit alignment audit is
presented in Table~\ref{tab:best-practices}.  Three status symbols are
used: a check mark (\checkmark) signals an explicit implementation,
a cross ($\times$) flags a known limitation that is not yet
addressed, and an asterisk ({\circledast}) denotes a partial or
informal alignment in which the underlying intent of the standard is
honoured but no formal compliance claim is made.

\input{tables/best_practices}

Three observations are warranted.  First, the granularity criteria
imposed by the corporate-accounting standards (GHG~Protocol Scope~2
location-based, RE100, 24/7~CFE) are met by construction: the
\cenpipe{} pipeline emits a per-bus per-quarter
$\varepsilon_{b,t}$ trajectory whose temporal resolution is finer than
any of the three schemes mandates.  Second, the market-based reporting
mode of the GHG~Protocol cannot yet be supported because the pipeline
does not ingest a Guarantees-of-Origin or I-REC ledger; the absence is
a deliberate scope choice and is flagged as future work in the
conclusions.  Third, the data-exchange standards (IEC~CIM/CGMES) are
not honoured at the schema level because the upstream PCP file is a
PLEXOS-specific XML dialect; a CIM serialisation of the reconstructed
network is a tractable extension whose design has been sketched but
not implemented.  In the verification dimension (ISO~14064-3) the
pipeline is auditable in the sense that every reconstructed
$\hat\lambda$ can be cross-checked against the PLEXOS PCP oracle
when the corresponding \texttt{.accdb} archive is available, and the
audit harness reports a median absolute discrepancy of less than
\SI{0.5}{\dollar/\mega\watt\hour} on
\SI{99}{\percent} of the cells in the validation window.  Compliance
with the operational ancillary-services rulebook (CEN
NT~SSCC~2024~\cite{CENNT_SSCC2024}) is direct: the SCVIC ladder
consumes the same per-fuel CV tariff structure defined in
NT~SSCC, which is what permits the closest-CV picker to operate on a
common cost basis.
