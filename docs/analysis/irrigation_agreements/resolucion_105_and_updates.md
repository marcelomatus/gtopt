# Resolución DGA N° 105/83 and Irrigation Agreements for the Laja and Maule River Systems in Chile

## Research Summary

This document compiles findings from web research on "Resolución 105" and related
regulatory/contractual frameworks governing irrigation agreements (convenios de riego)
in Chile's Laja and Maule river basins, including recent 2025-2026 updates.

---

## 1. Resolución DGA N° 105 de 1983

### 1.1 Identification

- **Full title**: "Resolución D.G.A. N° 105/83 y sus efectos en los derechos
  consuntivos del Río Maule"
- **Also known as**: Documento N° 15 — "Colbún y Río Maule"
- **Issuing authority**: Dirección General de Aguas (DGA), Ministerio de Obras
  Públicas, Chile
- **Year**: 1983
- **Length**: 16 pages
- **Subject**: Effects on consumptive water rights (derechos consuntivos) of the
  Río Maule resulting from the construction of hydroelectric plants

### 1.2 Context

The resolution was issued in response to the construction of the Colbún-Machicura
hydroelectric complex on the Maule river basin. This complex, which began operations
in 1985, was designed to serve dual purposes: ensure agricultural irrigation in the
region and harness hydraulic force for electricity generation. The resolution
addresses how the arrival of hydroelectric infrastructure affects the existing
consumptive water rights of irrigators.

### 1.3 Key Provisions

- **October-January delivery mandate**: The resolution mandates that between October
  and January of each year, 100% of the water rights must be delivered to each
  irrigator (regante).
- **Impact on irrigators**: The resolution is described by irrigator associations
  as having "gravely affected" the rights of irrigators due to the prioritization
  conflicts arising from hydroelectric generation.
- **Drought flexibility**: In practice, during drought periods the Junta de
  Vigilancia has sometimes distributed 80% instead of the mandated 100%, as a
  water-saving measure.

## 2. Maule River Basin: Irrigation Agreements

### 2.1 Key Actors

| Actor | Role |
|-------|------|
| **Junta de Vigilancia del Río Maule (JVRM)** | Irrigator oversight board for the Maule basin |
| **Asociación Canal Maule (ACM)** | Major irrigation canal association (~50,000 hectares) |
| **Colbún S.A.** | Operator of Colbún-Machicura hydroelectric complex |
| **Enel Chile** (formerly Endesa) | Operator of Pehuenche and other hydroelectric plants |
| **Dirección de Obras Hidráulicas (DOH)** | Government agency overseeing hydraulic works |
| **Coordinador Eléctrico Nacional** | National electrical system coordinator |
| **CNE (Comisión Nacional de Energía)** | National Energy Commission |

### 2.2 Convenio de Ahorro Hídrico Colbún-Pehuenche

- **First version**: 2008
- **Purpose**: Store irrigation water available in spring in the Colbún and
  Machicura reservoirs, for use in summer when river flows decrease
- **Duration**: Originally annual, extended to 5-year periods
- **Recent renewal**: Approved by the JVRM Assembly for renewal for **10 years**
  (circa 2025)
- **Water toll**: Irrigators above Colbún pay 30% for the use of the Pehuenche
  reservoir to Enel (stabilized from a higher rate to 70% paid by Enel)
- **Impact**: Approximately 400 million cubic meters of extra water made available
  for irrigation through integrated management with Colbún and Pehuenche

### 2.3 Laguna del Maule — Recovery and Current Status

- **Historic recovery**: After 14 years, Laguna del Maule recovered its upper
  level, accumulating **826 Hm3**, consolidating itself as the main multi-annual
  reserve of the basin
- **Effective reserve**: According to the Coordinador Eléctrico Nacional, the
  effective irrigation reserve is **838.2 GWh**
- **Coordinador reserves breakdown** (as of mid-2022): Colbún (69.11 GWh),
  Laja (104.63 GWh), Ralco (135.67 GWh), Maule (65.26 GWh) — total 374.67 GWh

## 3. Lago Laja: Irrigation Agreements

### 3.1 Convenio de 1958

- **Signed**: October 24, 1958
- **Approved by**: MOP Decree No. 2534, October 29, 1958
- **Parties**: Dirección de Obras Hidráulicas (DOH) and ENDESA (state-owned at
  the time)
- **Purpose**: Construction and operation of a reservoir on Lago Laja to ensure
  irrigation for agriculture in the lower zones of the basin, while assigning
  ENDESA the responsibility of administering reservoir operations
- **Administration**: ENDESA (now Enel) administers the reservoir under daily
  DOH supervision
- **Subsequent modifications**: Five additional changes after the 1990s
  liberalization
- **Key conflict**: The Asociación de Canalistas del Laja (2,100 members) has
  accused Endesa/Enel of "irrational" water use. Endesa has faced 16 legal
  claims for extracting more water than stipulated

### 3.2 Transitory Modifications (2014-2016)

- Starting in 2014, three annual agreements were signed for lake recovery
- One provision required Endesa to stop operating the **El Toro power plant
  (450 MW)** in the months before snowmelt to help reconstitute reservoir volumes
- In December 2016, a transitory modification was signed but was criticized as a
  failure by the Canalistas del Laja

### 3.3 Convenio Permanente de 2017

- **Signed**: November 2017
- **Parties**: Ministerio de Obras Públicas (MOP) and Enel Generación Chile,
  ratified by canal associations
- **Character**: Permanent (indefinite), reviewable if conditions change
- **Key innovation**: The use criteria shifted from fixed volumes to
  **percentages** based on the reservoir level (cota). This determines whether
  priority goes to generation or irrigation on a monthly basis
- **Flexibility**: Allows monthly scheduling of withdrawals, unlike the previous
  annual volume approach
- **Coordination**: Required participation of the Coordinador Eléctrico Nacional
  for generation scheduling
- **El Toro constraints**: Central El Toro (Enel) must generate a minimum of
  **80 MW** to fulfill irrigation commitments with the Canalistas del Laja
  associations during irrigation season. Power variations are allowed if
  compensated such that daily total water volume extracted equals the volume
  requested

## 4. Key Legal Milestones

### 4.1 Corte Suprema Ruling (March 2021)

After eight years of litigation, Chile's Supreme Court ratified the validity of
the 1947 convention, ruling against ENEL:

- **Agriculture has priority** over hydroelectric generation for the use of
  Laguna del Maule waters.
- Only surplus water ("sobrantes eventuales") may be used for electricity
  generation via run-of-river plants.
- The ruling (70 pages) confirmed that "economies" (water savings) generated in
  Laguna La Invernada can only be used when Laguna del Maule is in its upper
  portion (i.e., at high levels).
- The court held that the convention's primary object is "dar mayor seguridad
  al riego" and generation is secondary.
- The intermediate reserve (~500 Hm3) is allocated **80% for irrigation, 20%
  for generation**.
- All appeals by ENEL were rejected.

Sources:

- [Revista Electricidad — Corte Suprema mantiene fallo (2021-03-17)](https://www.revistaei.cl/2021/03/17/corte-suprema-mantiene-fallo-que-acogio-demanda-por-uso-de-aguas-de-la-laguna-del-maule/)

### 4.2 Cámara de Diputados Investigative Commission (2023)

The Chilean Chamber of Deputies approved an investigative commission (December
2022) to examine who authorized ENEL to use Laguna del Maule waters for purposes
other than irrigation, potentially contravening the 1947 convention.

After five months of work, the commission concluded that **there was no breach
of the 1947 convention**: when there is no water in the upper portion of the
lagoon, there is no obligation of water return by the DOH, and therefore no
violation of the agreement.

Sources:

## 5. Recent Updates 2025-2026

### 5.1 Maule Basin: Severe Water Deficit (2025-2026 Season)

The 2025-2026 irrigation season is one of the most deficient in decades:

- **Exceedance probability**: 96%-97% (only 3-4% of years have flows this low)
- **September-November flows**: 1,775 Hm3 (71% of total season)
- **December-March projection**: Only 709 Hm3 (an 80% drop from the prior quarter)
- **Basin deficit**: 60% as of early 2026
- **Spring savings**: Represent approximately 50% of available water

### 5.2 Convenio Colbún-Pehuenche Renewal

- The water-saving agreement was renewed for **10 years** (approved by JVRM
  Assembly, circa 2025), after the previous 5-year term
- Without the Laguna del Maule recovery combined with the Colbún-Pehuenche and
  ENEL-JVRM agreements, the basin would have faced a critical scenario with risk
  of not sustaining committed water distribution

### 5.3 Mesa Técnica Laguna del Maule (2026)

A Technical Working Table has been established with three sessions held:

- **Participants**: JVRM, DOH, ENEL
- **Objective**: Define an Operating Model for the Laguna del Maule reservoir
  based on clear, verifiable, and sustainable rules
- **Key activities**:
  - Simulation exercises to evaluate **emergency flow curves** to safeguard
    irrigation security
  - Analysis of scenarios with several consecutive deficit years
  - **Volume correction**: DOH study suggests the stored volume in Laguna del
    Maule may be less than currently published; update of available data is
    recommended
  - DOH presented an exploratory model analyzing reservoir volume evolution under
    different hydrological and **climate change scenarios**
- **Next steps**: Focus on mechanisms to increase water supply, reduce pressure
  on Laguna del Maule, and improve operational coordination

### 5.4 JVRM New Operational Proposal (January 2026)

The JVRM Board presented a new proposal for operating Laguna del Maule:

- Complements the Colbún-Pehuenche savings agreement
- Aims to strengthen integrity and efficiency in managing the basin's water
  resources
- Presented to DOH professionals and Enel's area manager (Claudio Helfmann)

### 5.5 Coordinador Eléctrico — Flexibilization of Water Reserves

The Coordinador Eléctrico Nacional has requested flexibilization of water reserves
in reservoirs after rains and snowmelt forecasts:

- Maule and Laja are mixed-use reservoirs, with irrigation reserves defined by
  the irrigators of the respective basins
- Generation rights subject to water reserve formation in Lago Laja can be used
  until November 30
- In Laguna del Maule, the deadline is conditioned by construction-related works

### 5.6 Laja Basin: 2025 Status

- Main reservoirs in the Biobío region at 39% of capacity (as of March 2025)
- Total stored volume approximately 2,600 million cubic meters
- The 2017 permanent agreement continues to govern operations

## 6. Complementary Legal and Regulatory Framework

### 6.1 Water Rights Law

- Chile's Water Code (Código de Aguas) governs water rights
- Key distinction: **consumptive rights** (derechos consuntivos, for irrigation)
  vs. **non-consumptive rights** (derechos no consuntivos, for hydroelectric
  generation — water must be returned to the river)
- 2022 reform (Nuevo Código de Aguas): Introduced additional protections and
  usage requirements

## 8. Relevance to Power System Modeling (gtopt)

These irrigation agreements directly affect hydroelectric generation modeling:

1. **Minimum generation constraints**: El Toro must maintain 80 MW minimum during
   irrigation season; these translate to minimum power constraints in the LP model
2. **Water reserves**: The Coordinador defines energy reserves (GWh) in reservoirs
   subject to irrigation agreements, affecting hydro dispatch optimization
3. **Seasonal flow profiles**: The October-January 100% delivery mandate and
   spring savings patterns create time-varying constraints on hydro generation
4. **Multi-use reservoir modeling**: Colbún, Machicura, Laguna del Maule, and
   Lago Laja require modeling of dual irrigation/generation objectives
5. **PLP/gtopt integration**: The University of Chile thesis specifically studies
   how the Laja irrigation agreement affects long-term planning (PLP model),
   directly relevant to gtopt's optimization engine

## 9. Note on "Resolución 105"

The search confirmed that "Resolución 105" specifically refers to **Resolución DGA
N° 105 de 1983**, issued by the Dirección General de Aguas. It is NOT a resolution
from the CNE or the Coordinador Eléctrico Nacional. The resolution addresses the
effects on consumptive water rights of the Río Maule caused by the construction of
the Colbún-Machicura hydroelectric complex.

No separate "Resolución 105" from the CNE or Coordinador Eléctrico was found in
the research.
